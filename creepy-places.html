Screaming Tunnel

Location: Niagara Falls, Ontario, Canada

Hundreds of years back, a little girl hid in this tunnel to escape from her drunken father. The soul of the girl is said to reside inside the tunnel. If you walk into the tunnel with a wooden match, the girl apparently screams her heart out as if someone is after her.

Underwater City

Location: Shicheng, China

Also known as the “Lion City” that dates back to almost 1,400 years old. It was flooded by the Chinese government in 1959 in order to make way for a new hydroelectric power station. The ancient city is hidden 130 feet underwater.

The Tower of London

Location: London, England

A 900 year old castle and fortress that held many famous and infamous prisoners. It is haunted by many ghosts and one famous ghost seen is Anne Boleyn. She was one of Henry VIII wife who was imprisoned and executed. Witnesses see her beheaded body wandering around with her head. Other ghosts include two princes, Edward and Richard, age 12 and 9. They were evidently killed by their uncle Richard III who took over the throne. You can hear them giggle and witness them in their nightgowns.

Catacombs of Paris

Location: Paris, France

Sixty-five feet below the streets of Paris, 6 million people were buried to rest throughout 180 miles of labyrinth like tunnels. Only a small portion of the catacombs are opened to tourist. The rest is only accessible through secret passageways hidden throughout the city of Paris.

Military Hospital

Location: Beelitz, Germany

This is the hospital where Hitler was treated for injuries in WWI. It was a treatment facility that was shut down after the downfall of East Germany. All that is left is an abandoned building with the smell of disinfectant lingering and some of the equipments rusting inside.

Hell Fire Club

Location: County Dublin, Ireland

Located at Montpelier Hill, this was a hunting lodge built by Speaker Conolly. Conolly is said to have destroyed the cairn (large stone) while building the lodge. After some time the roof of the lodge was blown off and many believed that the spirits were seeking vengeance for destroying the cairn. After Conolly death, this was the beginning of the Irish Hellfire Club founded by Richard Parsons. The lodge was a place of for heavy drinking and amoral behavior. Members of the club were speculated to be Satanists and Devil-worshipers. The place remains burnt-out and abandoned with reports of unusual smells, traces of satanic rituals.

Aokigahara Suicide Forest

Location: Aokigahara, Japan

Known as the Sea of Trees, it’s a forest that is super thick and the trees organically twist and turn. Due to its location at the base of a mountain, the ground is uneven, rocky, surrounded with hundreds of caves. The trees are so tightly packed that wind cannot make its way in creating a sense of isolation and stillness. This is one of the most popular suicide destination in the world. Some believe the ghosts are vengeful and they torment visitors trying to lure those who are sad and lost off path.

The Hill of Crosses

Location: Šiauliai, Lithuania

A pilgrimage site that is full with hundreds and thousands of holy crosses. People from around the world visit this scared hill and leave behind a holy cross to fulfill their wishes. The hill was a place for Lithuanians to pray for their country and loved ones who sacrificed themselves in the wars of independence. Legend says that a Lithuanian farmer built a large wooden cross and placed it on the hill. The farmer had a daughter who was ill and about to die and every night the farmer would sit beside her and pray. He had a dream of a woman dressed in white telling him to follow her instructions to make his daughter better. The cross built was supposedly a sign of faith and love for God healing his daughter. The farmer’s daughter was cured after he placed the cross there.

The Island of Dolls

Location: Xochimilco, Mexico

Legend says that a little girl drowned here mysteriously and all the dolls are possessed by her spirit. Don Julian Santana Barrera was the caretaker of the island and found the little girl. He saw a doll floating near the canal and figured it belonged to the girl and as respect, he hung it to the tree. Julian was apparently haunted by the spirit of the girl and started hanging more dolls in order to please her spirit. After collecting dolls and hanging them for 50 years, he was found dead on the island at the exact spot where the girl drowned. The dolls apparently move their hands and whisper to each other.

Lizzie Borden’s House

Location: Fall River, Massachusetts

This is the house where Lizzie Borden and her family lived. She apparently killed both her parents, Andrew Borden and Abby Borden with an ax. Investigations found a hatchet with a broken handle in the cellar of the home. Lizzie was charged with murder of her father and stepmother however there was no conclusive proof that the hatchet was the murder weapon. Also the burning of Lizzie’s dress that may or may not have been worn on the day of the murders wasn’t enough to convince the jury. Till this day, the Borden murders remains unsolved. The house has been turned into a bed and breakfast for tourist.
